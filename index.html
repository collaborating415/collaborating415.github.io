Vote for positive leadership and a rejuvenated Local 415!

Elections for our union executive are Tuesday, December 3rd and this time you have a choice.

College counsellors, librarians, and faculty are facing serious issues. We need a new collaborative approach based on open dialogue and inclusivity. Together, we can move forward! It’s time to renew OPSEU Local 415.

Collaboration • Communication • Consultation

This is our opportunity to give change a chance!

Who?

The five members who have put their name forward are supported by a large group of like-minded members. Shawn, Cath, Paul, Roger, and Jen are all long-time faculty who possess the required skills to meet the needs of the membership and to successfully negotiate on your behalf.

What?

We are campaigning in efforts to:

  1. Strongly represent the membership based on your expressed needs and concerns
  2. Improve communications between the local and the membership
  3. Renew OPSEU Local 415 with an active membership committed to the cause (solving issues)
  4. Bring a mandate that aims to resolve members’ concerns at stage one
  5. Bring an annual review process that ensures the accountability of the local executive to its membership

Why?

The current approach is no longer an effective resolution model.

  • Meetings hosted by management receive greater faculty attendance than meetings held by the local
  • Faculty want a better resolution process
  • Faculty want to be informed if a grievance goes in on their behalf
  • The demands for counselling services has increased and its team has not grown proportionally
  • Management is making one-size fits all decisions that impact faculty without consultation with the local (examples: e-text, mobile learning, and AODA)
  • The Woodroffe working area is not subdivided into separate zones which created an imbalance in representation. Through our recruitment efforts we aim to increase the number of stewards so that the entire membership has representation at LEC ?

The opportunity exists now to change this situation to a more collaborative, consultative approach within our membership, the support staff local and with management.

How?

By getting your voice heard! – We were nominated with the support of several stewards because of our experience and our ability to represent the membership as a whole. We know it is possible to move forward by:

  • Re-instating the Union College Committee as per Article 7 of the Collective Bargaining Act. (Currently we are the only Ontario College without this committee)
  • Collecting members’ opinions and concerns as per Article 6 of our local Bylaws.
  • Being proactive on issues and by negotiating solutions developed in consultation with the membership.
  • Implementing a decision making process that supports members designed on the principles of collaboration, transparency, and open dialogue
  • Being strong advocates and committed protectors for each member by following the negotiated Collective Agreement and by participating in the upcoming negotiations
